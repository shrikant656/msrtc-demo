Meeting of MSRTC officers to discuss on transport service in Nashik

A meeting of senior Maharashtra State Road Transport Corporation (MSRTC) officers will be held in Mumbai on Monday to discuss the its transport service in Nashik.The division has already intensified its services in rural areas and has cut down numbers of tours on non-profit routes in the city areas"The MSRTC is facing losses of Rs 10 per km in the city and of Rs 2 per km in the rural areas. We have therefore been asking the Nashik Municipal Corporation (NMC) to share the losses or profits, but there is no response from the civic body. As a result we are unable to invest in buying new buses and putting them into service in the city," a senior officer from MSRTC said.

Reduced fares of point-to-point bus service

The Maharashtra State Road Transport Corporation (MSRTC) has reduced fares of point-to-point bus service also known as Shivneri Corporate from Rs 140 to Rs 100 between western suburbs - Kandivali to Bandra Kurla Complex. The reason behind this move is believed to be low patronage to the service.
